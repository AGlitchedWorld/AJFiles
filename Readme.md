# THE AJ FILES
### Story Mode

The 19th of June welcomed the morning sun at its usual time, for there is no reason why the rising of the sun should be anyhow affected by the petty ongoings on this terra firma. However, the happenings of that day for many of us were far, very far from petty.
The morning sun, however, was welcomed by very few in my class - being a Friday, it was mandatory for half the class to be absent under the guise of "self-preparation" - making the room brighter than usual.
I, however, was welcomed (summoned rather) by my incharge to his office early in the morning - making the morn darker than usual. Well, anyone being summoned by their higher-ups always fears the worst for no one knows what reason (of course, there are always those optimistic/leadership guys who seem to know enough to hold local versions of TED talks on their own) and though I had nothing to fear immediately, I was confused.

It so happened that the previous week, some of us were graciously bestowed with additional assignments of what they claimed were super-hard XL-500 papers, along with some previous years' Mains papers. We were, on the directions of our incharge, whom we fondly refer to as Himmler, made to sit and write them as an actual exam ("...just like the simulations...") with proper time (and improper invigilation). Halfway through the papers, after more head banging than solving, our physics sir (MB, epitome of kindness) rescued us by canceling the exam and allowing us to solve it freely at our pace. Imagine the reaction on Himmler's face when he learned of this. Imagine then the reactions on our faces the next day when our weekend exam had no less than twelve questions from those very papers. Same options, and even the same order of questions! It is a different matter that most of us stashed the papers in our bags after MB's rescue and didn't bother opening it until after the exam, that too out of bewilderment. Turns out it wasn't a leak after all, as per their claims - the branch responsible for setting the paper was given the responsibility a mere 48 hours before the exam itself, and so they flicked many questions from our previous year papers. It is another different matter that the branch responsible for setting the paper was our own (although technically it wasn't - it was the other MHL branch, not CO, and our people claimed ignorance of this fact). In either case, to their defence, it wasn't exactly a leak since our performances weren't much changed.

Recalling this, and the fact that by the time I was summoned down not many other "toppers" had arrived (not that many more would arrive either - only three more), I calmed myself thinking it'd just happen again. Halfway through, the realisation hit (as it did last time too) that a leak is actually not good. 

His office is a place of many memories and many happenings - not all happy. As was the custom, I peeked in to recon the situation and, more importantly, his mood, only to discover 1. That unusually sombre face (more than usual), 2. He wasn't using his phone, 3. He wasn't sitting on his chair, but on the visitors' chair. With more questions following me, I entered. The reason for the last two observations was made clear in the form of our Dean sir sitting in Himmler's chair (the most fearsome man we've encountered without exception - Hitler for us).
Usually when this happens, what follows is a long discussion about marks, improvement, growth, ranks and so on. But I'd done pretty well in the past few weeks, so that theory was also discarded. Not much later, Himmler uttered the most astounding statement till date (which I translate, since I cannot exactly recall his epic accent), "Today, inform to me whatever non academic discussions are held by the teachers."
Obviously, I wanted to pry the why from him, but the Dean's presence had me resort to "Okay sir".

A few days prior, the chemistry class had turned sleepy again, but much more than usual since our sir (ex-)chad man, self-proclaimed and student community approved greatest chemistry teacher, his (ex-)greatness, AJ was teaching what was perhaps the most important topic for our exams. It was common for him to treat our class as his court, in his own words - he was Akbar, we were his ministers, and some among us the Navratnas. AS was one of them - the Tansen of our class. He wasn't exactly a gifted vocalist, and he had sung only two songs before AJ (and yet we love him, of course). One of them was Saiyaara: he's renowned across floors for his rendition of this. So continuing the tradition of the king wishing for music when he pleases, and in this case because the singer was also among the many lolling their heads about, AJ called upon him to sing any song. The 'any' was redundant. AS initially refused, pointing to Himmler's surveillance system - the camera in our class which he monitors as any fangirl monitors the latest season of Gilmore Girls. AJ assured him sanctuary, "You have me with you!". He also added something like "You dare refuse to do what I ask?". We really think it's the second statement which finally got the song rolling. This time, AS was asked (commanded) to sing it as loudly as possible, so that it would reach the floors above us and below us too - as if it were the last time he'd ask him to sing. And sing he did, amid countless cheers and laughs and endless applause. The whole class - if not the adjacent classrooms too (which, coincidentally, also had chemistry classes then) - had definitely woken up.
There were similar incidents in MB's classes as well, where we'd discuss about his life, our life, our faculty's life and what not.

With this in context, I feared any misunderstandings against AJ or MB. I suppose at that point, the whole class would've revolted if there was anything against them. I couldn't help myself from asking, "Sir, anything at all? Like even - " 
"Yes sir, everything whatever happens, you should tell. Yours only I'm trusting. "

Well, that cleared up so much! Slightly uneasy, largely subdued, I went back ("The Name's Bond" playing in the background) and took my place. After all, what else could I do? I was deciding whether to side with the management and thus truth, or with my teachers and thus convenience and what would be expected of me by my fellow classmates.
But that decision was made for me by an entirely unforeseen entity.

That day, MB did not arrive. He had skipped a study hour the previous evening too. AJ still had syllabus left to teach. He had not come the previous day either, and had left the class on the day before that much earlier than usual, too. We were dumped with worksheets instead, for physics and chemistry both.
Even as the fifteen of us were sitting in the absence of our physics master, taking the worksheet given more seriously than we did the previous week, time sped past at its own pace. Well, until it was break time. As usual, our guys ran to reserve the TT board (we say that there's more competition in our games room than in JEE), but as they didn't even make it out of the class - our class incharge (Mr. P) brought them to an abrupt halt, "Go to fourth floor, meeting is there."

Adding to my list of surprises, the auditorium contained nearly our whole administration till the second-highest tier- Mr. P, Hitler, Himmler, our AGM sir, and most notably, our CEO for Karnataka state division, S sir. We had interacted with him before only twice casually, all other encounters were related to something either dramatic or detrimental. 

We were all made to sit on either side of the long table, S sir at the head and a projector screen opposite to him. 
He began with a banger: "There are many upcoming online coaching programmes, like PW. Many people call them startups - but that's what gen-z people call it. We millennials have a different word for them: frauds."
He narrated the rapid rise and the devastating downfall of another such "educational venture": Byju's - how they misled investors by quoting projected asset values rather than the actual brand value, amassing a large number of investments, now left with a larger number of debtors.
Some of us were already guessing at it, albeit very wildly. A few of us had some extra data: the previous evening, Himmler had called LS's parents and ranted on about how ed-tech startups were trying to lure in our exceptionally good faculty, and even our exceptionally competent students. It wasn't hard to connect the dots; digesting the resulting picture was another matter entirely...

He continued, "You all know how much we all love and respect AJ sir. He is a very good teacher, undoubtedly. Even MB - he's been with us for so long."

AJ was really highly respected. Not just among students, but the faculty and management too.
MB was in our institution for more than twelve years - the oldest surviving (thriving!) instructor over here. We learnt later that he'd even sworn an oath on his God that he would not abandon this ship no matter what storm may hit.
"You might also have seen many times, we have lunch together, we have coffee together... but in reality, something else is going on behind us."
"I should not be talking about such faculty members like this, but you all should know the truth."
S sir then instructed his technical operator to display something on the screen.
And whatever did we see....
